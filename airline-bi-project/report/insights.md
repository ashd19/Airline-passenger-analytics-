# Key Insights: Airline Passenger Satisfaction Analysis

## 1. Top Drivers of Satisfaction
The analysis reveals that service quality significantly outweighs operational delays in determining passenger satisfaction.
- **Online Boarding (20.4%)**: The most critical factor. A seamless digital boarding experience is the strongest predictor of a satisfied passenger.
- **In-flight Wifi Service (15.8%)**: Connectivity is no longer a luxury but a baseline expectation for modern travelers.
- **Travel Class & Type of Travel (24% combined)**: Business travelers and those in Business/First class have significantly higher satisfaction expectations and ratings compared to personal/economy travelers.
- **In-flight Entertainment (6.6%)**: Content quality and accessibility play a major role in the overall flight experience.

## 2. The "Delay Paradox"
Surprisingly, **Departure and Arrival Delays** have minimal direct impact on the overall satisfaction score (combined importance < 1%).
- **Insight**: Passengers are remarkably tolerant of delays if the service quality (boarding, wifi, entertainment) is high.
- **Trade-off**: Investing in service improvements may yield a higher ROI in satisfaction than expensive operational efforts to reduce minor delays.

## 3. Segment Performance
- **Business vs. Personal**: Business travelers are generally more satisfied, likely due to better amenities and priority services.
- **Returning Customers**: "Returning" customers (loyalists) show higher satisfaction levels, suggesting that loyalty programs or familiar service patterns are effective.

## 4. Model Performance
- **Random Forest** and **Decision Tree** models achieved high accuracy (~94-95%), indicating that passenger satisfaction is highly predictable based on service ratings.
- **Logistic Regression** (87.8%) serves as a strong baseline but fails to capture complex interactions between service features.
